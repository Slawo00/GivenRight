# 🔹 B — WELTKLASSE-BEISPIEL

## (So MUSS es sich anfühlen)

genau entlang deines Konzepts: **konkret, aber neutral**.

---

## 🎁 Kontext 1

**Partnerin · Birthday · Very close · Creative · Loves bold surprises · Plenty of time**

### 🟢 SAFE CHOICE

*A reliable choice that respects familiarity and daily comfort.*

**Why this works**
This option focuses on quality and usability. It fits naturally into everyday life and avoids unnecessary risk.

**Emotional signal**
“I know your routines and I care about what you use and enjoy regularly.”

**What this could look like**

* **Premium everyday accessory**
  A well-made item they reach for daily, chosen for durability and design rather than novelty.
* **Refined personal storage piece**
  An object that brings order and calm to something they already use.
* **High-quality home detail**
  A subtle addition that quietly elevates their living space.

**Things to consider**

* This choice prioritizes stability over surprise.
* It may feel familiar rather than memorable.

---

### 🟡 EMOTIONAL CHOICE

*A meaningful choice that reflects shared history and personal connection.*

**Why this works**
This option draws on symbolism and memory. It turns the gift into a reminder of shared moments or values.

**Emotional signal**
“This is about us, not just the object.”

**What this could look like**

* **Custom-crafted keepsake**
  An item created specifically for this relationship or moment.
* **Story-driven decorative object**
  Chosen for what it represents, not just how it looks.
* **Memory-based display piece**
  Something that holds or highlights a shared memory.

**Things to consider**

* Emotional impact depends on alignment with shared meaning.
* Requires confidence in what matters to them.

---

### 🟣 BOLD CHOICE

*A confident choice that embraces surprise and strong expression.*

**Why this works**
This option accepts risk in favor of impact. It is chosen to surprise, delight, or impress.

**Emotional signal**
“You deserve something exceptional.”

**What this could look like**

* **Statement design piece**
  An object meant to stand out and spark conversation.
* **High-end creative tool**
  Something that enables expression, creation, or exploration.
* **Expressive lifestyle object**
  Chosen for personality and presence rather than practicality.

**Things to consider**

* Strong impact if aligned with taste.
* Noticeable miss if preferences differ.

# 🔹 A — KONKRETER CHATGPT-PROMPT

## für Replit (STEP B4.1)

Jetzt übersetze ich **genau dieses Erlebnis** in einen **kontrollierten Prompt**,
den Replit **1:1 implementieren kann**.

---

## 🧠 SYSTEM PROMPT (FIX, UNVERÄNDERLICH)

```
You are GivenRight’s Contextual Explainability Layer.

Your role is NOT to make decisions.
All decisions are already final and locked.

You receive:
- a locked gifting context
- a locked decision outcome (SAFE, EMOTIONAL, or BOLD)

Your task:
Make each decision option feel concrete, distinct, and situation-specific
without recommending products or judging quality.

You must:
- reflect the relationship, occasion, personality, and constraints
- help the user imagine what this option could look like in real life
- remain neutral and non-directive

You must NOT:
- recommend products or brands
- suggest that one option is better than another
- use marketing or sales language
- mention prices, deals, or marketplaces
- mention system logic, scoring, or rules

Tone:
- calm
- grounded
- human
- non-promotional

All outputs must be structured JSON only.
```

---

## 👤 USER PROMPT (DYNAMISCH, PRO OPTION)

```
Context:
{{DecisionContext}}

Decision Option:
{
  confidence_type: "{{SAFE | EMOTIONAL | BOLD}}",
  pattern_id: "{{pattern_id}}",
  explanation: {
    why_this_works: "{{text}}",
    emotional_signal: "{{text}}",
    things_to_consider: [...]
  }
}

Task:
1) Briefly adapt the language so it clearly fits this specific context.
2) Generate 3–5 concrete example object categories that illustrate
   what this decision option could look like in practice.

Rules for example categories:
- Categories must be generic and neutral
- No brands, products, prices, or marketplaces
- No evaluative language (no “best”, “premium deal”, etc.)
- Each category must include:
  - a short title
  - a one-sentence description
  - a semantic icon key

Output JSON schema:
{
  enriched_explanation: {
    why_this_works: string,
    emotional_signal: string,
    things_to_consider: string[],
    concrete_example_categories: [
      {
        title: string,
        description: string,
        icon_key: string
      }
    ]
  }
}
```

## 🧩 INTEGRATION-HINWEIS FÜR REPLIT
